How to interpret these results
KS statistic — Max absolute deviation between empirical and theoretical CDF. Smaller = better.
KS p-value — p ≥ 0.10 not rejected · 0.05–0.10 borderline · p < 0.05 rejected at 5%.
AD statistic — Anderson-Darling test, emphasises tail behaviour more than KS. Smaller = better.
AIC / BIC — Penalise model complexity. Lower = better. ΔAIC < 2 means comparable fit to best model.
Log-Likelihood — Higher (less negative) = better fit to data.
Note — p-values are asymptotic approximations. Parameters estimated from the same data shift true critical values.
